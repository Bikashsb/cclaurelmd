Cleaning Method & Process

What carpet cleaning method do you use?

We use truck-mounted hot water extraction at 220°F — the method recommended by the carpet industry and by major manufacturers including Shaw, Mohawk, and Stainmaster. Hot water at 220°F kills bacteria, dust mites, and their eggs, and removes allergens that dry cleaning or bonnet cleaning cannot address. Our cleaning chemistry is Green Seal GS-37 certified — eco-safe for families, pets, and the Patuxent watershed. Carpets are dry within 4–6 hours.

How long does carpet cleaning take to dry?

With our truck-mounted extraction equipment, carpets typically dry in 4–6 hours. Turning on fans and opening windows after cleaning helps. Rental machines from hardware stores leave carpets wet for 24–48 hours because of significantly lower suction power. In Laurel's humid summers near the Patuxent watershed, wet carpets left that long are at risk of developing mold under the pad. Our equipment removes moisture immediately.

Is steam cleaning the same as hot water extraction?

The terms are often used interchangeably. True steam cleaning uses dry steam vapor at very low moisture levels. Hot water extraction injects pressurized hot water at 220°F into carpet fibers and immediately extracts dissolved soil, bacteria, and allergens. Hot water extraction is the more effective method and what carpet manufacturers recommend for warranty maintenance. Both terms are commonly used in the Laurel MD market to describe professional carpet cleaning.

Do you move furniture before cleaning?
